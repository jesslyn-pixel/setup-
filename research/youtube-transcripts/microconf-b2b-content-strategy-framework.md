## Why This Video
Rob Walling (founder of Drip, runs MicroConf) breaks down a practical B2B content strategy framework using the 5 Stages of Customer Awareness. Directly relevant to how B2B SaaS companies should think about YouTube and content as an acquisition channel at each stage of the buyer journey.

## Key Takeaways
1. **5 Stages of Customer Awareness** (Eugene Schwartz, 1950s) applied to B2B SaaS content:
   - Stage 1: Unaware — doesn't know they have a problem
   - Stage 2: Problem Aware — knows the problem, doesn't know solutions
   - Stage 3: Solution Aware — knows solutions exist, not specific products
   - Stage 4: Product Aware — comparing your product to competitors
   - Stage 5: Most Aware — ready to buy, needs final push
2. **Start at the bottom (Stage 5)** — create content for buyers closest to purchasing first, then work upward
3. **YouTube fits Stage 1-2** — broad educational content ("10 email marketing tips") works for unaware/problem-aware audiences
4. **Content at every stage looks different** — top of funnel = YouTube/blog, bottom = case studies, versus pages, long-form demos
5. **Even outbound sales teams need content** — at stages 4-5, content becomes sales literature that closes deals

00:00:00 even if you aren't doing content marketing this talk should and will apply to you... even if you're doing outbound sales... towards the latter two stages of the five you need content even if you don't think about it as content marketing.

00:00:34 2012-2013 story: paid $5-6K for content before launch of Drip (then called Velvet Mail). Launched content, crickets. Reason: no strategy or framework for prioritizing content — all top-of-funnel, generic posts. "Seven email marketing myths, a beginners guide to email marketing" — too high in the funnel.

00:02:17 Framework: take the 5 stages of customer awareness (concept from the 1960s) and apply it to B2B content marketing.

00:04:39 Stage 1 — Unaware: prospect doesn't realize they have a problem. In B2B, dangerous territory — requires outbound education. YouTube videos like "10 email marketing tips" work here but conversion is very slow.

00:05:47 Stage 2 — Problem Aware: knows they have a problem, doesn't know solutions. Searching "how do I send bulk email" not "MailChimp." Best content: templates, guides solving immediate problems (not product-specific).

00:06:30 Stage 3 — Solution Aware: knows categories exist (email marketing software) but not specific products. This is where Capterra, G2, Reddit, Quora come in. People searching "best email marketing software."

00:07:09 Stage 4 — Product Aware: comparing your product to competitors. Versus pages, alternative-to pages, case studies. Even if you're only doing outbound sales — this content helps close deals.

00:08:00 Stage 5 — Most Aware: ready to buy. Needs features (not just benefits), long-form demos (30-45 min), case studies, security/compliance pages, knowledge base.

00:09:01 Key insight: "Start at the bottom" — create Stage 5 content first (it helps close sales immediately), then work upward toward Stage 1.

00:13:00 Tools for monitoring Reddit/HN when people mention your product: F5bot and Syften. Google Alerts is "useless these days."

00:19:00 Capterra ads work well for Stage 3-4 prospects — already solution-aware, high conversion rates despite high CPC.

00:25:56 "Content maybe isn't marketing now, it's just sales literature" — at Stage 4-5, content serves both marketing and sales teams.
