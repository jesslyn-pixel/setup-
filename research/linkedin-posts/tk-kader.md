# TK Kader - LinkedIn Posts

## Post 1: Pre-launch founders are stuck

- Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tkkader_most-saas-pre-launch-founders...
- Topic: Pre-launch mistakes / validation

Summary:
TK highlights that many SaaS founders get stuck before launching because they overthink product ideas instead of validating demand. He emphasizes that progress comes from engaging real users early.

Key Takeaways:
- Overthinking kills momentum
- Talking to users > building in isolation
- Validation should happen before product development
- Speed matters more than perfection

## Post 2: Founders overcomplicating SaaS

- Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tkkader_every-saas-founder-i-talk-to...
- Topic: SaaS strategy / simplicity

Summary:
TK points out that many founders are making SaaS more complicated than necessary. Instead of focusing on solving one clear problem, they try to build overly complex solutions.

Key Takeaways:
- Simplicity wins in SaaS
- Clear problem > complex solution
- Many founders confuse features with value
- Focus improves execution speed

## Post 3: $119B raised but still failing

- Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tkkader_startups-raised-over-119-billion...
- Topic: Startup funding / reality check

Summary:
TK highlights that despite massive funding in the startup ecosystem, many companies still fail. He argues that funding does not replace strong fundamentals like product-market fit.

Key Takeaways:
- Funding ≠ success
- Product-market fit is critical
- Many startups fail despite capital
- Execution and validation matter more than money
